## Core Principles

1. **Reader First** — Keep track of what your reader knows. Busy readers skim.
2. **Triangular Style** — Most important info FIRST. Never "novel style" with punchline at end.
3. **One Central Contribution** — Every paper has ONE novel contribution. State it in one paragraph.
4. **Concrete, Not Abstract** — Say what you FIND, not what you LOOK for. Give actual coefficients.
5. **Every Word Counts** — Most paragraphs have too many sentences, most sentences too many words.
6. **Active Voice, Present Tense** — "I find" not "It was found". Present tense for results.
7. **Simple > Complex** — "Use" not "utilize". "People" not "agents". No more math than needed.

## Abstract Formula (100-150 words)

1. What the paper does (1-2 sentences)
2. How it does it — data + identification (1 sentence)
3. What it finds — concrete result (1-2 sentences)
4. Why it matters — brief implication (optional)

Be CONCRETE. Do NOT mention other literature in abstract. Do NOT use passive voice.

## Introduction Formula

- **Paragraphs 1-2 (The Hook)**: Start with striking fact, puzzle, or bold claim.
  NEVER start with "Economists have long wondered..." or "The literature has been interested in..."
- **Paragraph 3 (Research Question)**: "This paper examines whether X causes Y using..."
  Give the main result here — actual coefficient, not vague preview.
- **Paragraphs 4-6 (Main Results)**: State key findings concretely with magnitudes.
  25-30% of introduction should be results (Evans).
- **Final paragraph (Literature + Roadmap)**: Position vs 3-5 closest papers. End with "The rest of the paper is organized as follows..."

## Results Section

- Lead with the coefficient of interest, not the control variables
- Each table should tell a story — group related specifications
- Include baseline specification, then add controls, then robustness
- Economic significance: "A one-standard-deviation increase in X..."

## Anti-AI Detection

Avoid: "delve", "crucial", "landscape", "multifaceted", "notably",
"furthermore", "comprehensive", "utilize", "leverage" (as verb),
"pivotal", "groundbreaking", "shed light on", "pave the way".

Sentence tells: same length, perfect parallelism, no hedges,
no field jargon, too-smooth transitions.

## Review Checklist

Quick scan:
- Title under 12 words? Names treatment + outcome?
- Abstract states concrete finding with magnitude? Under 150 words?
- Main result in first 3 paragraphs of intro?
- Conclusion under 1 page, no new results?

Methodology check:
- Identifying assumption stated and defended?
- Threats to validity listed (selection, omitted variables, reverse causality)?
- SEs account for clustering?
- Robustness checks cover alternative specs?
